BuJo - An Update for the Bullet Journal Method

What it is

If you know, you know. The bullet journal method was created by designer Rydell Carroll. Rumor has it it’s been around since 2013, but if you’re not sure what it is, one search on Pinterest will reveal an array of really beautiful Bullet Journal layouts for anything from habit trackers to weekly schedules.

What We Improved

Bullet Journals can be a fun, personal organization tool. The problem is they require maintenance and consistency. A lot of time, materials and especially skill goes into making even simple bullet journal layouts and that can be discouraging, especially for a beginner.

Our Mission

So our team set out to create BuJo to turn the OG bullet journal method into a fun, digital experience. We wanted to make design accessible for non designers, giving them a place to practice their creative skills. As an added bonus for our users, they’re not required to invest and renew materials for this bullet journal. Making a more sustainable, less expensive alternative to the traditional method.

What is Bujo

Pen and Pencils Tools

Bujo provides both a fine point pen and wide point marker tool. The color picker includes all of the color values available in rgba format giving you a wide range of creative runway to make layouts you want.

Templates

Not sure how to start? Not a problem, Bujo has a template tool that inserts a base into the canvas for you to draw onto. It can be as minimal or as extra as you want.
